> **Rule of this project: every psychological claim the app makes is traceable to a real source and hedged to its real confidence level. We never fabricate citations, and we never overclaim.**

This file is the evidence spine for [`VISION.md`](VISION.md) and [`MASTER_PLAN.md`](MASTER_PLAN.md). Section numbers (§) are referenced from those docs. Confidence labels are deliberately conservative.

## §1 — Behavioral Activation (BA) for depression — **Strong**
- **NICE (2022). *Depression in adults: treatment and management*, NG222.** BA named among first-line psychological therapies. https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222
- **Cuijpers, P., van Straten, A., & Warmerdam, L. (2007).** "Behavioral activation treatments of depression: A meta-analysis." *Clinical Psychology Review*, 27(3), 318–326.
- **Individual BA meta-analysis (2023),** *Psychotherapy Research*. https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10503307.2023.2197630
- WHO angle: BA is a core strategy in **WHO Problem Management Plus (PM+)** and **mhGAP**. *(Anchor "WHO uses BA" to PM+/mhGAP — there is no standalone WHO BA guideline.)*
- **Finding:** BA is a first-line, guideline-recommended depression treatment; scheduling rewarding activity is as effective as CBT or medication.

## §2 — Social connection & mortality — **Strong**
- **Holt-Lunstad, J., Smith, T. B., & Layton, J. B. (2010).** "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk: A Meta-analytic Review." *PLoS Medicine*, 7(7), e1000316. **~50% greater survival odds (OR ≈ 1.5); 148 studies, ~308k people.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2910600/
- **Holt-Lunstad, J., et al. (2015).** "Loneliness and Social Isolation as Risk Factors for Mortality." *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 10(2), 227–237. **Isolation OR 1.29; loneliness OR 1.26; living alone OR 1.32; ~3.4M people.** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1745691614568352
- **Finding:** Weak connection raises mortality risk ~26–32%; strong connection ~50% better survival odds — comparable to major physical risk factors.

## §3 — Expressive/reflective writing — **Moderate**
- **Pennebaker, J. W., & Beall, S. K. (1986).** "Confronting a traumatic event." *Journal of Abnormal Psychology*, 95(3), 274–281. *(Origin of the paradigm.)*
- **Smyth, J. M. (1998).** *J. Consulting & Clinical Psychology*, 66(1), 174–184. **Average d ≈ 0.47.**
- **Frattaroli, J. (2006).** "Experimental disclosure and its moderators." *Psychological Bulletin*, 132(6), 823–865. **Smaller overall effect (r ≈ 0.075)** — cite for honesty.
- **Pennebaker, J. W. (2018).** *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 13(2), 226–229. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1745691617707315
- **Finding:** Small-to-moderate, heterogeneous benefits. Present the journal/Chamber modestly.

## §4 — Rituals reduce anxiety/grief, improve performance — **Moderate**
- **Norton, M. I., & Gino, F. (2014).** "Rituals alleviate grieving for loved ones, lovers, and lotteries." *J. Experimental Psychology: General*, 143(1), 266–272. *(Mediated by restored sense of control.)* https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/norton%20gino%202014_e44eb177-f8f4-4f0d-a458-625c1268b391.pdf
- **Brooks, A. W., et al. (2016).** "Don't stop believing: Rituals improve performance by decreasing anxiety." *OBHDP*, 137, 71–85. https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/Rituals%20OBHDP_5cbc5848-ef4d-4192-a320-68d30169763c.pdf
- **Finding:** Deliberate ritual lowers grief and performance anxiety via a restored sense of control. Emerging, lab-based — label as such.

## §5 — Self-compassion — **Strong**
- **Neff, K. D. (2003).** "The development and validation of a scale to measure self-compassion." *Self and Identity*, 2(3), 223–250. *(Defines "common humanity.")*
- **MacBeth, A., & Gumley, A. (2012).** *Clinical Psychology Review*, 32(6), 545–552. **r = −0.54** with depression/anxiety/stress. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22796446/
- **Zessin, U., et al. (2015).** *Applied Psychology: Health & Well-Being*, 7(3), 340–364. **r ≈ 0.47** with wellbeing.
- **Neff, K. D. (2023).** *Annual Review of Psychology*, 74, 193–218. https://self-compassion.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Neff-2023.pdf
- **Finding:** Strongly linked to less distress and greater wellbeing. Directly supports the "rough stone, not broken" stance.

## §6 — ACT / values-based living — **Strong**
- **Hayes, S. C., Strosahl, K. D., & Wilson, K. G. (2012).** *Acceptance and Commitment Therapy* (2nd ed.). Guilford.
- **A-Tjak, J. G. L., et al. (2015).** *Psychotherapy and Psychosomatics*, 84(1), 30–36. **Comparable to CBT.**
- **Gloster, A. T., et al. (2020).** *J. Contextual Behavioral Science*, 18, 181–192. **20 meta-analyses, 133 studies, 12,477 participants — efficacious across all conditions.** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2212144720301940
- **Finding:** Values + committed action have robust support. Grounds the Plumb.

## §7 — PERMA — **Mixed (heuristic only)**
- **Seligman, M. E. P. (2011).** *Flourish.* Free Press.
- **Butler, J., & Kern, M. L. (2016).** "The PERMA-Profiler." *Int. J. Wellbeing*, 6(3), 1–48. https://www.internationaljournalofwellbeing.org/index.php/ijow/article/view/526
- **CAVEAT — Goodman, F. R., et al. (2018).** *J. Positive Psychology*, 13(4), 321–332. **PERMA subscales correlate ~1.0 with a single wellbeing factor — the five pillars are not empirically distinct.**
- **Finding:** Use as a practical lens. Our Three Pillars (Wisdom/Strength/Beauty) are a *heuristic* whole-person model, explicitly **not** a validated factor structure.

## §8 — Paced breathing & HRV — **Strong physiology / moderate anxiety**
- **Laborde, S., et al. (2022).** *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews*, 138, 104711. Slow breathing reliably raises HRV/vagal activity. https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0149763422002007
- **Lehrer, P. M., & Gevirtz, R. (2014).** *Frontiers in Psychology*, 5, 756. ~6 breaths/min ≈ 0.1 Hz resonance maximizes baroreflex/HRV.
- **Zaccaro, A., et al. (2018).** *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 12, 353. Slow breathing ↔ relaxation, reduced arousal.
- **Finding:** Target ~6 breaths/min for The Level. *(Prefer the resonance/slow-breathing evidence over "box breathing," which has thinner RCT support.)*

## §9 — Progress, mastery, SDT — **Strong**
- **Ryan, R. M., & Deci, E. L. (2000).** *American Psychologist*, 55(1), 68–78. Autonomy/competence/relatedness.
- **Ryan, R. M., & Deci, E. L. (2017).** *Self-Determination Theory.* Guilford.
- **Howard, J. L., Slemp, G. R., & Wang, X. (2024).** SDT meta-analysis — all three needs predict motivation/wellbeing. https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/2024_HowardSlempWang_Meta.pdf
- **Amabile, T. M., & Kramer, S. J. (2011).** *The Progress Principle.* HBR Press. Small wins are the strongest booster of "inner work life." https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=40692
- **Finding:** Degrees & streaks are justified **only** when framed as competence/progress/autonomy — never as shame or compulsion.

## §10 — Meaning/purpose vs. mortality reflection — **Split**
Purpose (**strong**):
- **Frankl, V. E. (1946/2006).** *Man's Search for Meaning.* Beacon.
- **Hill, P. L., & Turiano, N. A. (2014).** *Psychological Science*, 25(7), 1482–1486. **Higher purpose → lower all-cause mortality over 14 yrs.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4224996/
- **Steger, M. F., et al. (2006).** *J. Counseling Psychology*, 53(1), 80–93.

Mortality-salience (**contested — flag**):
- Terror Management Theory (Greenberg, Pyszczynski, Solomon) — 500+ studies, **BUT**:
- **Many Labs 4 (Klein, R. A., et al., 2022), *Collabra: Psychology*** and **Sætrevik & Sjåstad (2022), *PLOS ONE*** — **failed to replicate** the core mortality-salience effect.
- **Finding:** Build the Chamber around **meaning, values, and what matters now.** Memento-mori imagery is philosophical framing, never an evidence claim.

## §11 — Freemasonry, social capital, men's mental health — **Weak/absent for the Craft itself**
- **HONEST VERDICT: no credible empirical research links Freemasonry specifically to mental-health outcomes.** Do not claim it. Use the analogs below.
- **Putnam, R. D. (2000).** *Bowling Alone.* Simon & Schuster. Documents the collapse of fraternal orders (names Freemasonry, Elks, Lions, Rotary) as declining social capital. *(Describes decline/civic cost, not member clinical outcomes.)* https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/articles/bowling-alone-americas-declining-social-capital/
- **Men's Sheds (strongest "brotherhood" analog):**
  - **Foettinger, L., et al. (2022).** "The Role of Community-Based Men's Sheds in Health Promotion for Older Men." *American Journal of Men's Health*, 16(2). Counteracts isolation/loneliness; wellbeing benefits substantiated. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/15579883221084490
  - Milligan et al. (2016); Wilson & Cordier (2013) — foundational Men's Sheds reviews.
- **Finding:** Frame Freemasonry as a *structural/historical exemplar* of the belonging and social-capital mechanisms that Putnam and the Men's Sheds evidence actually support. The mechanism is real; the Craft is the metaphor.
