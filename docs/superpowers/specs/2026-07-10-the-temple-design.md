# The Temple — a wages-and-courses progression system

**Status:** DRAFT design spec for review (brainstorm capture). Not yet committed to the repo; not yet planned.
**Date:** 2026-07-10
**App:** The Ashlar Protocol (fully on-device, zero-cost, no backend/accounts/internet, research-grounded)

---

## 1. What this is

A long-arc progression system: **daily / weekly / monthly research-designed challenges** that pay **wages**, which you **lay** to raise your **Temple** — an edifice of **50 courses**. Each course you raise **unlocks a real practice or tool** (the course *is* curriculum) and can be **adorned** (personalized). It gives the founder the three things they asked for — a felt sense of progress, a reason to return daily, and a genuine earn-and-spend economy — **without** the one mechanic the evidence condemns for this audience: an extrinsic points/coins reward loop.

**The pun that is the whole design:** a **course** is both *a horizontal layer of stone in a wall* and *a unit of study*. 50 courses = 50 layers of your Temple = 50 units of a real curriculum.

---

## 2. Why it's shaped this way (the evidence)

A five-thread research pass (2016–2026 meta-analyses, RCTs, and the best-evidence gamified MH apps) produced a strikingly convergent verdict:

- **Points/coins/levels add ~nothing to mental-health *outcomes*.** Head-to-head (same app, gamification on/off): Six et al. 2021 (*JMIR Mental Health*, 38 studies, n=8,110) — apps reduced depression (g=−0.27) but gamification was **not a significant moderator** of symptoms (β=−0.03, p=.38) or adherence. Nishi et al. 2024 (34 RCTs, n=5,119) — adding game layers bought a **"trivial"** +489 steps/day. → *This is an engagement bet, not an outcomes bet. We frame it honestly as such.*
- **Coins/virtual currency are the single riskiest element, and worst for this audience.** No MH RCT isolates currency as helpful. Overjustification (Deci, Koestner & Ryan 1999, 128 experiments): tangible, expected, contingent rewards **crowd out** intrinsic motivation (d≈−0.40) — the exact motivation recovery depends on. Variable/earned-reward loops are gambling-adjacent (Zendle & Cairns 2018: loot-box mechanics correlate with problem gambling ~13× more than fixed ones) — a real hazard for the app's addiction-recovery users. → *No coin economy, no random rewards.*
- **But the *structure* the founder wants is exactly what the RCT-winners did — minus the coins:**
  - **SPARX** (Merry 2012, *BMJ*): remission 43.7% vs 26.4% (NNT≈6). **Seven levels = seven CBT skills. No points, no coins.**
  - **SuperBetter** (Roepke 2015): depression d=0.67→1.05 at follow-up; the *quest + identity structure* carried the effect, nearly content-agnostic.
  - **eQuoo** (Litvin 2023): adherence **64.5% vs ~20%**; the win was **one-level-per-week, non-bingeable unlocking + real skills**, not its "gem shards."
  - Recurring winning ingredients: **progression-as-curriculum, narrative identity, real-world challenges (behavioral activation), paced forgiving cadence.**
- **The challenge cadence is defensible when challenges are real actions:** behavioral activation (Ekers 2014, SMD −0.74), implementation intentions (Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006, d=0.65), and a never-reset streak (Lally 2010: one miss barely dents automaticity; range 18–254 days).
- **Perfectionism/shame guardrail:** streak-loss and "must-do" challenges recruit perfectionistic concern (Limburg 2017, r≈.40 with depression) and manufacture the Abstinence Violation Effect (Marlatt & Gordon). → *Challenges are invitations; nothing is ever punished or taken.*

**What we are explicitly NOT building** (and why): a spendable coin **economy of extrinsic reward** (overjustification), **random/variable rewards** (gambling-adjacency), **breakable streaks / streak-freezes** (AVE, monetized anxiety), **leaderboards / social comparison** (equity harm; also impossible on-device). The founder's "spendable economy" survives only in the specific, safe form below.

## 3. The core mechanic: wages → courses → Temple

**Wages.** The fruit of real work, themed as the Craft's wages — *corn, wine, and oil* (the Fellowcraft's wages in the Middle Chamber lecture). One quiet resource. Earned **only** by doing challenges and tending the stone. **Deterministic** (fixed, predictable amounts — never random), **never real money.**

**Laying (spend = build).** You invest wages to **raise the next course** of your Temple. The wage is spent — the balance goes down — but it becomes **permanent stone in your building**. *Spent, never lost.* This is the design's keystone: it is a genuine earn-and-spend loop that structurally **cannot** become a scarcity trap or a slot machine, because what you "spend" only ever *accumulates as visible edifice*.

**Course = curriculum.** Raising a course **unlocks a real, research-grounded practice or tool** (SPARX/eQuoo model). No empty XP thresholds. If a course doesn't teach or unlock something genuine, it doesn't ship.

**Adorn.** Wages can also be laid into **personalizing** the Temple/Lodge (Compose-drawn, on-device) — self-expression, "make it mine."

**Quiet numbers.** The wage counter is deliberately understated. **The Temple rising is the display** — progress you *reveal*, not a score you watch (the evidence says surface mastery, don't dangle a counter).

## 4. How it unifies with the existing app (the stone ↔ the Temple)

The app's current heart is the **rough ashlar** — your *self*, smoothed by daily tending; the *being* dimension, never complete. The Temple is the *mastery* dimension — the *doing*, the craft you build over 50 courses. These are not two competing metaphors. The Masonic resolution unifies them:

> **The perfect ashlar is the stone that gets fitted into the Temple.**

So: **daily tending smooths your stone (self); the smoothed stone + your wages are laid as the courses of your Temple (mastery).** One coherent arc — the self-work you already do *becomes the material* you build with.

Concretely, it rides on what's already built:
- **Degrees** (Apprentice → Fellowcraft → Master Mason) become the **three major thresholds** across the 50 courses.
- **Tending-the-stone** (kind, never-reset streak) stays as the **daily heartbeat** and a wage source.
- **Tools / Chamber** become the **craft you unlock** course by course.
- **"What the Stone Remembers"** (the mirror) **narrates the Temple rising** — a natural new fact/noticing surface.
- **The Board** gains a **Temple** surface (the edifice visual + today's challenges).

## 5. The three challenge tiers

All research-designed, all forgiving, all wage-paying.

- **Daily — a small, optional menu.** Behavioral-activation micro-actions (pick one value-aligned action). An **invitation**, never a mandatory must-do; a missed day carries zero shame and takes nothing.
- **Weekly — the real unit.** One value-aligned challenge that is the week's spine (where the therapeutic dose lives; weekly beats a rigid daily grind). Non-bingeable pacing (eQuoo).
- **Monthly — reflection, not a task.** Aggregate the month into a "what the stone remembers"–style review; folds into the existing mirror.

**Challenge content** is drawn from the app's research spine (the F1–F8 dossier: implementation intentions, environment design/The Cornerstone, behavioral activation/The Gauge, cognitive work/The Plumb, connection/The West Gate, the Rough Edge anti-addiction track, sleep rhythm, self-compassion). **Fixed cue, rotating content:** keep the implementation-intention anchor constant, vary the challenge on top of it for novelty.

## 6. The 50-course curriculum (framework + phasing)

**50 done right ≈ 50 genuine micro-competencies.** eQuoo taught 52 real skills, so 50 is legitimate — *only if each course is real.* The failure mode is filler; we design a curriculum, not a number line.

**Structure:** the 50 courses are grouped under the three degrees as thresholds — e.g. **Apprentice ≈ courses 1–17** (foundations: showing up, environment, implementation intentions, self-compassion, the daily gauge), **Fellowcraft ≈ 18–34** (deeper craft: cognitive work, connection, rhythm, the rough edge), **Master Mason ≈ 35–50** (integration, maintenance, teaching-back, values). *(Exact split is a build detail.)*

**A course contains:** a name (thematic), a real skill/practice it teaches or a tool it unlocks, its research basis (cited), the challenge(s) that build it, and the wages to raise it (tuned so a course is achievable within ~a week of genuine engagement — never grindy).

**Phasing (critical):** **do not author 50 hollow levels.** Build the **framework + the Apprentice tranche** first (the system, the Temple visual, wages, daily/weekly challenges, ~the first degree's courses), prove the loop motivates on-device, then author Fellowcraft and Master Mason in tranches. This is also the cheapest test of the biggest unknown (does the build-the-Temple loop actually motivate?).

## 7. Locked guardrails (non-negotiable)

1. **Core tools + §9 crisis are NEVER gated behind wages or courses.** "Unlock craft" = *new/advanced/optional* content only. Any tool someone might need in a hard moment is always free and reachable. Locking therapy behind currency would be a real harm; this line is absolute.
2. **Wages only accrue and get laid** — never deducted into scarcity, never decay, no "streak-freeze" to buy back, no wallet that runs low and blocks you.
3. **Deterministic only** — no random drops, mystery boxes, jackpots, or variable payouts. (Anti-gambling, protects the addiction audience.)
4. **A missed challenge takes nothing** — invitation, not obligation; no red badges, no shame copy, no sad-mascot notifications.
5. **Never-reset** — the daily heartbeat stays the kind, monotonic tending streak; no fragile consecutive-day streak a single lapse destroys.
6. **No real money, no leaderboards, no social comparison.** Fully on-device; the whole system is **disable-able** without losing any therapeutic function.
7. **Honesty line:** never marketed as "50 levels cures you." Levels/challenges are engagement + identity scaffolding around research-grounded content (Six 2021).

## 8. Architecture sketch (consistent with the app)

Pure logic + data + UI, on-device, following existing patterns:
- `tools/Temple.kt` — pure: the course ladder (definitions, degree thresholds), `wagesFor(challenge)`, `costToRaise(course)`, `courseState(...)`. Deterministic, trivially testable.
- `tools/Challenges.kt` — pure: the daily/weekly/monthly challenge catalog + selection (fixed-cue/rotating-content), `isAvailable(...)`, non-punitive completion.
- `data/*` — `@Serializable` models (WageLedger append-only; CourseProgress; ChallengeCompletion), DataStore JSON/int stores.
- VM StateFlows + a `Temple` Board surface (Compose canvas for the rising edifice; adornment as Compose-drawn layers).
- Every user-facing string added to the `SafetyAuditTest` corpus (mortality + willpower/grit gates).
- Wage/course reads follow the **DataStore-source-of-truth** rule (read `dataStore.X.first()` in snapshot logic, not cold `WhileSubscribed` StateFlows — per the mirror fix, PR #32).
